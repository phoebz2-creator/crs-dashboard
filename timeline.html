Overview

This page collects key events in the June 2025 Iran–Israel conflict and highlights the major turning points that changed the trajectory of the crisis. It is designed as a research companion to the policy dashboard, using news agencies, official nuclear-monitoring sources, and policy analysis outlets as reference points.

Current Status: As of June 2025, active military operations have largely paused under a ceasefire and de-escalation framework. However, regional tensions remain high, and long-term political and security disputes remain unresolved.

While a ceasefire was reached, debate continues regarding the long-term impact on Iran's nuclear program, regional deterrence, and U.S. involvement in the conflict.

Key Turning Points

  1. June 13: Israel’s opening strikes transform the shadow conflict into open war.
  2. June 22: U.S. strikes on Iranian nuclear sites raise the stakes dramatically.
  3. June 23: Iran’s strike on Al Udeid risks direct U.S.–Iran escalation.
  4. June 23–24: The ceasefire announcement and acceptance create the first diplomatic off-ramp.
  5. Post-strike assessments: Disputes over nuclear damage shape the longer-term policy debate.

Timeline

June 13, 2025
Turning Point

Israel launches major strikes on Iran

Israel begins a large-scale operation against Iranian military and nuclear-related targets. The strike marks the shift from long-running shadow conflict to direct military confrontation.

Why it matters: This is the opening escalation that turns the crisis into a direct Israel–Iran war.

Sources: Reuters; Associated Press; BBC News

June 13–14, 2025
Retaliation

Iran launches missile and drone attacks toward Israel

Iran responds with missile and drone attacks, bringing Israeli population centers and air-defense systems directly into the conflict.

Why it matters: The conflict becomes a two-sided exchange rather than a one-off Israeli strike.

Sources: Reuters; AP

June 14–21, 2025
Escalation

Repeated exchanges deepen the conflict

Israel and Iran continue strikes over several days. Regional governments and global markets monitor the risk of a wider war and possible disruption to Gulf energy flows.

Why it matters: The war expands from a short shock into a sustained crisis.

Sources: Reuters; BBC News; Financial Times

June 22, 2025
Turning Point

United States strikes Iranian nuclear sites

The United States enters the conflict by striking Iranian nuclear-related facilities, including Fordow, Natanz, and Isfahan.

Why it matters: U.S. military involvement changes the war from a bilateral Israel–Iran conflict into a direct U.S.–Iran confrontation risk.

Sources: Reuters; New York Times; Washington Post

June 22, 2025
Nuclear Risk

IAEA reports no unusual off-site radiation levels

Following strikes on nuclear-related sites, the IAEA reports that no increased off-site radiation levels had been detected.

Why it matters: Nuclear contamination risk becomes a central concern for Gulf states and international monitors.

Sources: IAEA; Reuters

June 22, 2025
Nuclear Facilities

IAEA says Isfahan tunnel entrances were hit

The IAEA states that entrances to underground tunnels at Iran’s Isfahan nuclear complex, associated with enriched uranium storage, were impacted.

Why it matters: The status of Iran’s enriched uranium stockpile becomes a major post-strike question.

Sources: IAEA; Reuters

June 23, 2025
Turning Point

Iran strikes Al Udeid Air Base in Qatar

Iran retaliates against the United States by launching missiles toward Al Udeid Air Base in Qatar, a major U.S. military hub in the Gulf.

Why it matters: This creates the clearest risk of direct U.S.–Iran escalation, but also opens space for a calibrated off-ramp.

Sources: Reuters; Associated Press; BBC News

June 23, 2025
Turning Point

Trump announces Israel–Iran ceasefire

U.S. President Donald Trump says Israel and Iran have agreed to a “complete and total” ceasefire.

Why it matters: This is the first clear diplomatic break in the fighting.

Sources: Reuters; Associated Press; New York Times

June 24, 2025
Ceasefire

Israel accepts U.S. ceasefire proposal

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu’s office says Israel has agreed to the U.S. ceasefire proposal after achieving its stated operational goals.

Why it matters: The ceasefire moves from announcement to official acceptance.

Sources: Reuters; Associated Press

June 25, 2025
Assessment

Questions emerge over damage to Iran’s nuclear program

Reporting based on preliminary U.S. intelligence assessments raises questions about whether U.S. strikes destroyed Iran’s nuclear capability or only delayed it.

Why it matters: Competing damage assessments shape the political debate over whether the war achieved its strategic goals.

Sources: Reuters; CNN; New York Times
